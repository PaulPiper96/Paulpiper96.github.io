3D Design

My interest in 3D graphics began when I took a Maya course at university, and I quickly became captivated by 3D modeling. I started using Maya in 2018, and from there, I transitioned into the Substance Suite. In 2020, I ventured into Blender, particularly exploring its sculpting mode, which ultimately led me to learn Zbrush. As I initially focused on classic hard-surface modeling and later delved into organic forms, I made the decision to learn Fusion 360 in 2022

Maya, Blender, Zbrush, Substance Suite, Fusion 360, Unreal Engine

Logo Designs


I began my journey into art by taking drawing classes in high school, with a primary focus on comic art. Over time, I transitioned into classical graphic design, especially in creating logos. During my college years, I dedicated more attention to graphic design and honed my skills, particularly by learning Adobe Illustrator, which remains my favorite tool for crafting logos

Illustrator, Procreate

Interfaces

Given my strong background in development through formal education, I naturally cultivated a significant interest in designing interfaces and interactions. I believe that having a grasp of coding is a substantial advantage for a designer, as it prevents the creation of overly complex concepts. Additionally, it enables me to integrate design principles from the early stages of development, making web development and design seamlessly interconnected fields for me.

Adobe xd, Photoshop, Illustrator
